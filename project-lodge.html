Bear Mountain Lodge Project

Spring 2019

Architectural Design I

This project was the final project for my Architectural Design I class. The assignment was to create an Appalachian Trail Shelter to be placed near the summit of Bear Mountain, CT.

The shelter was required to hold sleeping space for at least 30 people, as well as spaces for recreation, food preparation, and sanitation. The final design was hand drawn and presented on thick poster board and a model of the structure was made out of chipboard.

This project taught me how to design for the uses of a structure and the topography of the area. It also helped me better understand how to incorporate aspects of point, line, and plane into a useful and dynamic design.
