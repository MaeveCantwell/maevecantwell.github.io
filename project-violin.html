Sound of the Violin

Spring 2020

Grant-Funded Research

This project built on the work of a previous student. I worked with one other student and the project was funded by a University of Hartford Faculty-Student Engagement grant.

The purpose of the project was to investigate the mode shapes of the top plate of a violin by simulating Chladni patterns. The simulation results were compared to physical violin Chladni patterns.

The previous student had simulated Chladni patterns on a flat violin plate. This research focused on refining the 2D geometry, curving the geometry into 3D, and simulating the properties of wood in order to produce a more accurate model.
